The metaverse, which was originally only a concept of science fiction described as a virtual reality, separate, but mirroring that of our own, has undoubtably become more real. With the rapid development of XR (VR, AR, MR, etc.) technologies, an industry that has been rising alongside the advancement of AI, there comes a lack of a design framework for this medium. To prepare for future applications towards the metaverse, it is important to define the concepts that encompass the simulated reality: the visual construction of the virtual world and its exploration via user input. The virtual reality of the metaverse is designed using 3D computer graphics and software for building scenes inhabited with NPCs and player avatars. Furthermore, for this to be possible, users should be able to perform an array of XR-assisted interactions such as touching and grabbing, panning, point-and-teleporting, and walking just to name a few. User actions, their feedback, and immersion is only a small portion of what there is to consider as XR grows towards the metaverse.

Zhao, Yuheng et. al. "Metaverse: Perspectives from graphics, interactions and visualization." Visual Informatics, vol. 6, no. 1, 2022, pp. 56-67, https://doi.org/10.1016/j.visinf.2022.03.002. Accessed 28 Sep. 2025.
